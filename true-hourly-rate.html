üí° What This Means For You

If your true rate is below $25/hour: You're underpricing significantly. Consider raising rates or reducing unpaid time (streamline admin, reduce prep).

If your true rate is $25-40/hour: You're in the average range for private music teachers, but there's room to optimize. Look at reducing expenses or raising rates modestly.

If your true rate is $40-60/hour: Great! You're pricing well for a skilled professional. Focus on maintaining quality and student retention.

If your true rate is $60+/hour: Excellent! You've found a profitable model. Consider whether you can scale or increase rates further based on demand.

Action Step: Compare your true hourly rate to your local cost of living and professional goals. Would you accept this hourly rate in any other skilled profession? If not, it's time to adjust.
