üéØ Why This Matters

Should you charge $35 for 30 minutes or $65 for 60 minutes? Offer package deals or charge per lesson? Charge more for advanced students? These aren't just random decisions‚Äîeach pricing model fundamentally changes your income, workload, and business sustainability.

Most music teachers set one price and never test alternatives. But your pricing strategy directly impacts three critical factors: your hourly rate, your student retention, and your market competitiveness.

The Reality: A 45-minute lesson at $50 might seem like more money than a 30-minute lesson at $40, but when you factor in prep time and scheduling gaps, the shorter lesson could actually earn you more per hour. Similarly, monthly packages can increase cash flow predictability but might reduce per-lesson rates.

This calculator lets you compare different pricing models side-by-side so you can see which strategy maximizes your income while staying competitive in your market.

üí° Making Smart Pricing Decisions

True Hourly Rate Matters Most: Don't just look at the lesson price‚Äîfactor in prep time. A $70/hour lesson with 20 minutes of prep might earn you less per hour than a $40/30-minute lesson with 5 minutes of prep.

Longer Isn't Always Better: Sixty-minute lessons sound premium, but they're exhausting and limit scheduling flexibility. Many teachers find 30-45 minutes ideal for maintaining energy and maximizing student count.

Package Pricing Pros/Cons: Monthly packages provide predictable cash flow and reduce no-shows, but slight discounts reduce per-lesson earnings. Worth it if you value stability.

Competitive Research: Survey local teachers' rates for your instrument and experience level. Pricing 10-20% below market gets you students fast; pricing at market builds sustainable income; pricing 10-20% above market requires exceptional reputation or unique value.

Test Pricing Changes: Apply new rates to NEW students first. This lets you test without alienating existing students. If 5+ new students accept the rate without hesitation, you're priced correctly (or could go higher!).
