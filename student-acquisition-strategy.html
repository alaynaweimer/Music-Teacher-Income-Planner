üí° Making Your Marketing Work

Conversion Rates Are Real: Not everyone who inquires becomes a student. Expect 30-50% conversion from inquiry to first lesson. Factor this into your outreach goals.

Consistency Beats Intensity: Better to post in one Facebook group twice a week for 3 months than blast 10 groups once. Build relationships, not spam.

Track Everything: Ask every new student "How did you hear about me?" Use the Referral Tracker tool to identify your best channels and double down on what works.

Referrals Are Gold: A referred student converts at 60-80% (vs 30% for cold leads) and stays longer. Always ask happy families if they know anyone looking for lessons.

Free Channels First: Exhaust word-of-mouth, community groups, and school partnerships before paying for ads. These build sustainable, long-term student pipelines.

üéØ Why This Matters

Most music teachers approach student acquisition backwards. They blast marketing everywhere, hoping something sticks. Or worse, they do nothing and wonder why the phone isn't ringing.

The reality is this: your marketing strategy should match your student need. If you only need 3 more students, posting in Facebook groups and asking current families for referrals is enough. If you need 20 students, you'll need a multi-channel approach with paid advertising.

The Problem: Teachers waste time and money on marketing channels that don't match their goals. They pay for Google Ads when word-of-mouth would suffice. Or they rely solely on referrals when they actually need aggressive outreach to fill their schedule.

This tool creates a customized acquisition plan based on how many students you need, factoring in realistic conversion rates and timelines. No more guessing‚Äîjust a clear roadmap to hit your student target.

üí° Marketing Strategy Deep Dive

The Conversion Rate Reality: Not everyone who contacts you becomes a student. Word-of-mouth referrals convert at 60-80%. Facebook groups 20-40%. Paid ads 10-30%. Plan your outreach accordingly.

Start Free, Scale Paid: Always exhaust free channels (referrals, Facebook groups, school partnerships) before spending on ads. Free channels also build sustainable pipelines that keep generating students.

Consistency Over Intensity: Posting in 1 Facebook group twice weekly for 3 months beats blasting 10 groups once. People need to see you regularly to trust you.

Track Your Sources: Ask EVERY inquiry "How did you hear about me?" Use the Referral Tracker to identify your best channels and focus energy there.

Timeline Realism: Word-of-mouth is slow but sustainable. Facebook groups show results in 2-4 weeks. Paid ads can work immediately but are expensive. Build a mix for steady growth.
